Outreach

Al The Science Gal

At the height of the 2020 Coronavirus pandemic, I was looking for a way to continue the outreach efforts that I had been so passionate about during my undergraduate career. Thus, my YouTube Channel, "Al the Science Gal" was born. Here, I post videos about my experience in STEM, as well as professional developement videos. Moving forward, I hope to document my experience in graduate school, interview other graduate students, and discuss diversity in STEM, amongst other things.

National Technical Institute for the Deaf Mentoring Program

Did you know that in the past 20 years only 7 Ph.D.s were awarded to Deaf individuals worldwide? Based on the Deaf US population statistics, we should expect 3-4 deaf graduate students, and 3-4 deaf faculty members in Astronomy. As a result of this dispartiy, Prof. P. Muirhead and Prof. J. Nordhaus (National Technical Institute for the Deaf) are working to breaking down barriers for deaf and hard-of-hearing students in astronomy by creating a summer research internship program at Boston University. As life begins to return to normal, I will work with Prof. Muirhead and Prof. Nordhaus to create a peer mentorship program between graduate students at Boston University and undergraduate students at NTID. These mentors will support the NTID students both during and after their time as summer research interns.

Spooky Lab- Haunting at the Museum

Each year, The University of Alabama Museum of Natual History hosts an event around Halloween for families to come and learn about "spooky" science. As co-founder and vice president of The University of Alabama Society of Physics Students, I involved the physics department in the event for the first time. The SPS executive board planned and executed the entire event. Families were able to come and and learn about the science behind "ghosts" (hankercheifs over floating magnets), "spooky scientist hair" (Van der Graaf generator), "Halloween's cradle" (Newton's cradle with plastic pumpkins), and much more!.

Conference for Undergraduate Women in Physics 2019

The APS CUWiP is a series of regional conferences held simultaneously around the United States and Canada for undergraduate women interested in physics.In January 2019, The University of Alabama hosted the Conference for Undergraduate Women in Physics. In the year leading up to the event, the local organizing committee (of which I was a part of) met to plan the activities, speakers, poster session, panel discussions, etc. for the event. This event allowed women and gender minorities to network with people of all ages and professional levels, discuss graduate school and career oportunities, as well as learn about the cutting-edge science being done by women across the field.
